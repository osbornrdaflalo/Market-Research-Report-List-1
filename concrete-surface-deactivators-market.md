<p><ul><li>Organic Agents</li><li>Inorganic Agents</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Concrete surface deactivators are classified into two main types: organic agents and inorganic agents. Organic agents typically consist of natural compounds that slow down the curing process of the surface layer, allowing for easier removal and enhancing aesthetic appeal, such as color and texture. Inorganic agents, on the other hand, are chemical compounds that react with the cement matrix, effectively controlling the hardening process to expose aggregates and create a desired finish. Both types serve to improve concrete surface quality and longevity.</p></p>
