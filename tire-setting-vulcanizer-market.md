<p><ul><li>Mechanical</li><li>Hydraulic</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The tire setting vulcanizer market can be categorized into mechanical and hydraulic types, each serving unique applications. Mechanical vulcanizers utilize simple lever systems to apply pressure, often suited for smaller operations or less complex tire shapes. In contrast, hydraulic vulcanizers employ hydraulic systems for greater pressure and control, making them ideal for high-volume production and complex tire designs. Both types focus on curing rubber compounds under heat and pressure to ensure durability and performance in finished tires.</p></p>
