<p><strong>The Plasma Expander Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Dextran</li><li>Hydroxyethyl Starch</li><li>Human Albumin</li><li>PEGylated Albumin</li><li>Polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP)</li><li>Gelatin</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The plasma expander market comprises several key types used to increase blood volume in patients. Dextran is a polysaccharide that enhances vascular volume but may cause allergic reactions. Hydroxyethyl Starch (HES) is synthetic and effective for volume expansion but has safety concerns. Human Albumin is a natural protein used for various clinical conditions. PEGylated Albumin improves stability and circulation time. Polyvinylpyrrolidone (PVP) is a synthetic polymer with similar uses, while gelatin is derived from collagen and is considered a safer alternative.</p></p>
