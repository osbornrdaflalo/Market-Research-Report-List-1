<p><p>Automatic Power Factor Controllers (APFCs) are devices that manage and optimize the power factor in electrical systems, improving energy efficiency and reducing penalties from utility companies. The market includes two main types: Active APFCs and Passive APFCs. Active APFCs use controlled electronic systems to dynamically adjust reactive power, ensuring optimal performance in varying load conditions. Conversely, Passive APFCs rely on fixed capacitor banks or inductors to correct power factor but may be less flexible. Both types are essential for industries seeking improved energy management.</p></p>
