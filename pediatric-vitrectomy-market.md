<p><ul><li>Pars Plana Vitrectomy</li><li>Anterior Vitrectomy</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The pediatric vitrectomy market includes two primary types: Pars Plana Vitrectomy and Anterior Vitrectomy. Pars Plana Vitrectomy involves surgical removal of the vitreous gel through the pars plana region of the eye, commonly addressing issues such as retinal detachment and vitreous hemorrhage. Anterior Vitrectomy focuses on the anterior segment, removing vitreous fibers to manage complications like trauma or cataracts. Both procedures are crucial in treating pediatric eye disorders, facilitating improved vision and eye health in children.</p></p>
