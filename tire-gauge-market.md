<p><p>The tire gauge market includes two main types: digital and analog. Digital tire gauges use electronic sensors to provide precise, easy-to-read pressure measurements on an LCD screen, often with additional features like backlighting and auto-shutoff. Analog tire gauges, on the other hand, rely on mechanical components, such as a dial or a sliding rod, to display pressure readings. While analog gauges are durable and cost-effective, digital gauges offer higher accuracy and convenience, catering to different user preferences and needs.</p></p>
